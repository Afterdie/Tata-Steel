To address the limitations of its mobile curative clinics, Tata Steel, in collaboration with Impact India Foundation and Indian Railways, hosted the Lifeline Express – the world's first hospital on rails, resulting in a permanent improvement in the quality of life for over 15,000 patients suffering from post-polio deformities, hearing disabilities, facial and eye disorders.

The Outdoor Patients Department of the 740-bed Tata Main Community Initiatives Hospital (TMH) in Jamshedpur sees an average of 2300 patients each day. TMH is complemented by nine dispensaries that serve an average of 2800 people each day. A specialised cancer hospital, a blood bank, 14 specialty clinics, and two de-addiction centres are also supported and managed by the company.

Rural Services, Tribal Services, and the Community Development and Social Welfare Department manage mobile health clinics to fulfil the needs of the greater community.
